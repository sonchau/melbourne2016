Guest Speakers

 

Rev Dr. Allan Meyer

Allan Meyer was born and raised in the Lutheran Church in Melbourne, Australia. At the age of 19 while studying Economics and Politics at Monash University and preparing for a career as a Secondary School Teacher he had a dramatic encounter with God and was called into the Ministry - a call that he had no desire to obey!

revival broke out in the life of the school. Many of the young people of that school found Christ through that outreach

He graduated with a Bachelor of Economics and a Diploma in Education and entered the teaching profession at Lilydale High School in Melbourne in 1970. It was at this High School that the call of God was to find its place in his life. The increasingly self-destructive and meaningless lifestyles of many of the Australian young people he was teaching provoked him to emerge from the closet as a Christian and thrust the claims of Jesus before the student body. He and his wife, Helen, opened their home to the students on Friday nights and a minor revival broke out in the life of the school. Many of the young people of that school found Christ through that outreach, some of whom are now serving in full-time Ministry in churches throughout Australia. Allan served as a teacher in that school for seven years until the burden of the call to ministry stirred him to resign and enter Bible College in 1977.

During that year of Bible College a large Pentecostal Church (Life Ministry Centre under the leadership of Ps Hal Oxley) called him to become their first Youth Pastor. Here he served for the next 5 years as part of the ministry team of that influential church in Melbourne - the whole of that time of ministry being spent pasturing, counseling and shepherding the Youth of both the church and Oxley College, the school established by Life Ministry Centre.

In 1983 Allan received a call from a local charismatic Church of Christ, Mount Evelyn Christian Fellowship, to become their Pastor. The church was renamed Careforce Church Mt Evelyn in 2003. Over the years the church increased to the place where it is home to around 2500 people.

a Ministry that releases healing, discipleship and evangelism in more than 2300 churches and organisations in over twenty countries around the world

In 1992 Allan, together with his wife, Helen, established Careforce Lifekeys (formerly known as Careforce Recovery Ministries), a Ministry that releases healing, discipleship and evangelism in more than 2300 churches and organisations in over twenty countries around the world.

Allan concluded his doctorate with Denver Seminary in 2005. His project produced a new program to restore and fortify the moral and spiritual integrity of men. This program for the sexual discipleship of men is known as Valiant Man.

In January 2009, after leading Careforce Church for 26 years, Dr Allan Meyer stepped into a role which allows him to focus on his primary passion - advocating the world changing Lifekeys ministry and preaching the biblical themes and insights that have touched lives through the Lifekeys ministry all over the world.

Allan is the author or the many manuals and teaching programs. His latest book is "From Good Man to Valiant Man".

Rev Le Thanh Trung

Rev Le Thanh Trung was born in a family blessed by God. Raised up in a Christian family, he began to learn how to love God since he was a child.

After the incident of 1975, during 20 years (1975 – 1995), he was trained by God in the environment of Da Lat Church, Vietnam where there was no pastor. During the next 20 years (1995 – 2015), he was used by God to shepherd a small church in Colorado Springs City, Colorado, USA.

Glory be to God in heaven forever!

Serving God is a way to express our gratitude and a respond to God’s gracious calling. With no degree or certificate, he serves simply because he is a servant that God can use. He asks God for wisdom because he knows that he is foolish. He trusts in God daily because he knows that he is weak.

Rev Le Thanh Trung shared: "It is a surprise and an honour for me to be a part in sharing God’s Words at Vietnamese Christians Fellowship Conference in Australia – Melbourne 2016. Glory be to God in heaven forever!"

Rev Le Thanh Trung is currently serving God in Colorado Springs City, Colorado, USA. His family includes Rev Le Thanh Trung, his wife, two sons, two daughters, two daughter-in-laws, two son-in-laws, and four grandchildren.

Rev Dr Joshua Duong

planting more than 150 home churches in North Asia and sending missionaries to South East Asia and beyond

Dr. Joshua Duong is the founder and president of Agape International Ministries, a mission-training and sending organizations empowered by God-given missions and visions to reach the nations for Christ. A former Associate Professor of De La Salle University and a Polytechnic University in North Asia, he was called to start Agape International Seminary in 2007 and up to date more than 600 young workers and thousands of leaders have been trained and sent into mission fields. There are more than 1000 young workers, leaders and pastors who are pursuing their theological, ministerial and mission training at the seminary and mission training centers currently.

He is also ministering to churches, conferences and crusades as revival, evangelistic and leadership speaker.

Through partnership his ministry also supports church planters and planting more than 150 home churches in North Asia and sending missionaries to South East Asia and beyond.

He is married to Dr. Mary F. a university professor and they are blessed with two wonderful boys, Luke and Joseph.

Widow of Rev Pham Xuan Thieu

Widow of Rev Pham Xuan Thieu, named Dang Thi Phuong Lan, was born in 1950 in Binh Thuy, Can Tho, VN. She is married to Pastor Pham Xuan Thieu on the 28th of April, 1974. Two months after April, 1975, the family including Rev Pham Xuan Thieu, his wife and their new-born son moved to Theological Seminary in Nha Trang, where Rev Pham Xuan Thieu was the president of the final year of the Seminary (1975 – 1976) before the Seminary was closed.

The whole family including one more 8-month-old son was ordered to move to pastor’s house of Nha Trang Church at the end of 1978 where Rev Pham Xuan Thieu was called to become Sunday School Teacher until he was called to become a Vice-President of Nha Trang Evangelical Church in 1982.

Until 1988, Rev Pham Xuan Thieu was called to teach in a newly opened Biblical College in Hanoi. Rev Thieu lived in Hanoi for five years (1988 – 1993). When the course finished, the college was closed. The whole family returned Nha Trang and during one year, Rev Thieu was not become a pastor for any church.

In 1994, the whole family moved to Saigon and lodged in a friend’s house for five years. At that time, Rev Thieu silently taught Bible for those who wanted to serve God in an exponential growth style, 1 become 5, 5 become 10...

to help the women in the churches have a holy life given by God

In 2001, he was elected to be the President of Christian Churches of Vietnam (HTTLVN) (Southern Vietnam). He was rest in the kingdom of God on the 22nd of June, 2002. After many years of serving God with the husband silently, in July, 2003, the Widow of Rev Thieu came to Singapore to pursuit the M.Div program at Trinity Theological College for three years to be able to continue serving God among the women. From 2006 until now, she cooperates the Ladies Group to help the women in the churches have a holy life given by God. Besides, she also an assistant teacher for The Theological Seminary.

Rev Thieu and his wife have two sons and six grandchildren.

Pastor Đoàn Hưng Thiện

Difficult childhood experiences led him to find God as a teenager. After experiencing a radical conversion, he entered into ministry.

Thien is originally born in Saigon, Vietnam and immigrated to the United States of America at the age of one. He grew up in a half Buddhist and half Catholic family. Difficult childhood experiences led him to find God as a teenager. After experiencing a radical conversion, he entered into ministry. A graduate of Bible College and of Talbot Theological Seminary, Biola University (MDiv, DMin-abd), he has been working in the area of church planting in various roles for more than a decade. He has personally planted three churches in Southern California and worked as assessor, coach, consultant and strategist for several church planting organization including the Acts 29 Network, Southern Baptist Convention, Evangelical Free Churches of America, and the LaunchStrong / Multiply Network.

He is a sought after speaker and has spoken and lectured at conferences and universities for The Gospel Coalition, Biola University, Golden Gate Theological Seminary, Acts 29 Network, The Reformed Church of America, and Union University of California. Currently he is the Lead Pastor of Citylights Church in Little Saigon, Orange County, which is among the first church plants targeting one of the least-reached people groups in North America.
